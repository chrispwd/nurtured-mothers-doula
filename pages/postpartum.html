Postpartum Support

Mothering the Mother

After a woman gives birth, it's a very fragile time. Often, a combination of hormonal changes, lack of support, inadequate food and exhaustion leave a new mother exhausted, lonely, or even depressed. 30%-50% of women experience postpartum mood disorders These numbers have continued to increase over the last several decades. It doesn't have to be this way.

With the proper reverance, support, and nourishment, many people find they come out of this sacred time rejuvenated. Postpartum is a unique biological period. During this window, the potential to heal is higher.

In traditional postpartum care, a doula is focused primarily on the mother. This can include warm and nutrient-dense food, emotional processing, bodywork, encouragement to rest, and occasional baby care. A nurtured mother is the perfect caretaker for her baby. ‚ù§
